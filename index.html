Momentum & Impulse

Momentum (p): Product of mass and velocity. Vector quantity. Formula: p = m * v

Impulse (J): Change in momentum caused by a net force applied over time. Formula: J = F * Δt = Δp

Newton's Second Law: F = Δp / Δt → net force equals rate of change of momentum.

Changing Momentum: Force and Time

  • To reduce force, increase the time of impact.
  • Examples: padded dashboards, boxer riding the punch.
  • Bouncing objects: greater impulse than stopping.
  • Impulse is greater when object changes direction.

Conservation of Momentum

  • Total momentum before = total momentum after.
  • Vector quantity: direction matters in collisions and explosions.
  • Newton’s Third Law: forces are equal and opposite → impulses equal and opposite.
  • Examples: rockets, firecrackers, collisions.

Types of Collisions

  • Elastic: Momentum & kinetic energy conserved.
  • Inelastic: Momentum conserved, kinetic energy lost.
  • Right-angle collisions: Equal momentum vectors → final motion at 45° if same mass.

Key Calculations

  • Momentum: p = m*v
  • Impulse: J = F*Δt = Δp
  • Inelastic collisions (sticking together): m1*v1 + m2*v2 = (m1+m2)*v_final
  • Elastic collisions or explosions: m1*v1 + m2*v2 = m1*v1_final + m2*v2_final
